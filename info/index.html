a bit about me

Welcome!#

      My name is Harshavardhan (Harsha) - I go to Duke, where I study math and computer science. I love learning anything that is useful to the world, but I’m especially interested in understanding how people think, and how to effectively make better decisions under risk, uncertainty, and incomplete information in general.

      One of my dreams is to build a platform for everyone, that can democratize uninterpretable decision-making tools so that they can complement our everyday decision-making. While it’s really popular to pursue big data and AI, I think a large part of the success of the next few decades will be measured by our ability to use these tools to alleviate suffering and improve our lives in an ethical and democratic way. I hope to make a small dent in this potential future.

      My background is in quantitative/algorithmic problem-solving, but I like to think that my life experiences molded me into a “specialized generalist” at heart - trying to recognize and marry interdisciplinary ideas and perspectives in the pursuit of a more holistic appreciation of the truth, whatever that may be. When doing this, I find myself generally drawn to heuristics, and often rely on its simplicity to make sense of uncertainty and complexity.

      I love moving people through storytelling - I actually think I’d be a performer in another world where money wasn’t a factor (most people wouldn’t pay to watch that). I really enjoy music - it’s a great way to build experiential memories. Strategy games like chess are also very fun.

      I’ve worked on and am working on some cool stuff during my time at Duke. Take a look if you feel so inclined.

      If you’re intrigued, get in touch - I love meeting new people.
